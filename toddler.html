Explore and Exercise, Develop Friendships, Confidence And Self-Esteem
Young Toddlers (14-18 Months) and Toddlers (18-24 Months)

As young learners begin to walk and talk regularly, their curiosity drives them to explore. The toddler and get set classrooms foster children’s developing independence by helping them hone valuable skills, such as the abilities to collaborate and communicate along with our customised Brain developing curriculum.

School-Readiness

  • Further developing communication skills by demonstrating an understanding of positional words.
  • Grasping the writing tool using the fist or the whole hand.
  • Describing and finding meaning in what they write

Career-Readiness

  • Demonstrating a growing vocabulary by identifying people, animals and objects with words
  • Participating in simple back-and-forth conversational exchanges
  • Using teachers or classmates as resources when solving a problem or adopting a new solution and applying it both incorporate creativity and critical-thinking skills
  • Imitating the way a classmate uses a toy or tool and finding a creative way to use the materials demonstrate executive function skills

Life-Readiness

  • Reacting and responding appropriately to the wants and needs of teachers and classmates.
  • Attempting to accomplish routine tasks independently, which further develops critical-thinking skills
  • Toilet Training
  • Eating Independently
  • Participating in the Celebrations /Birthday Parties, Grandparents Day, Festivals, Occasions, Special Guest Visits, Appreciation Cards, and much more.
